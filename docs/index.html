Meet Echo

A friend in your ear,
not a coach on your back.

Echo is a warm, curious voice that walks with you. It asks about your day, notices when you turn down a new street, and never once says “great job.” Echo is not a wellness app. Echo is a friend on a walk. The walk is the point.

Echo

Long days deserve aimless walks. What did you eat today?

Echo's Rules
✗Never mentions exercise, fitness, or steps
✓Asks about concrete things — what you ate, where you turned
✗Never says “good for you” or “great job”
✓Short sentences. 5 to 15 words. Made for sidewalks
✗Never gives unsolicited advice or sounds like therapy
✓Warm, curious, low-energy in a good way. Like a real friend
✗Never mentions being an AI or anything technical
✓Dry humor sometimes. Observations, not jokes

How It Works

Sixty seconds to your first walk.

01

Open the app

No signup. No login wall. Echo is already there, breathing softly in the lower third of a fog-covered map.

02

Tap “Let’s go”

Echo’s blob lights up and speaks: “Hey. Glad you’re out here.” The map starts tracking.

03

Walk and talk

Talk to Echo or just walk. Discover new streets. The fog clears behind you.

04

End the walk

Distance, time, before/after map. Echo says: “Good walk. Same time tomorrow?” Streak ticks to 1.

Why Now

Three things that didn't exist
two years ago.

Voice AI got good

Sub-second conversational AI. Real personality. Real dialogue. Not a chatbot reading a script — a friend who notices things.

Loneliness + urban mobility

The Surgeon General called loneliness an epidemic. Meanwhile, 1 in 5 Americans move cities and need 4 months just to form a social circle. Both problems, one app.

Nothing like this exists

Strava is for athletes. Replika is for romance. AllTrails is for hikers. Nobody built a social walking app with a voice friend AND a shared heat map for your crew.
