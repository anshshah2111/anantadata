## 1. AI Video Prompt (for Veo / Runway / Sora)

Use this as a single prompt or split into parts. Each `[SCENE]` is ~5 seconds. Total: ~30 seconds.

---

### Full prompt (give this to Veo as-is):

> **Style:** Cinematic, moody, dark color grading with warm amber accents. Realistic footage. No text overlays — purely visual storytelling. Gentle ambient soundtrack with soft piano.
>
> **SCENE 1 (0–5s):** A young professional (late 20s, hoodie, tired eyes) sits alone at a desk in a dim apartment. Laptop open, phone glowing. The only light comes from screens. Outside the window, golden afternoon light is fading. They don't look at it. Camera slowly pushes in on their face — flat, not sad, just... empty.
>
> **SCENE 2 (5–10s):** Same person, same apartment, different angle. A clock on the wall shows 5:30 PM. They glance at their phone. A warm amber glow pulses gently on the screen — like something breathing. They pause. Something caught their attention.
>
> **SCENE 3 (10–15s):** Cut to: shoes going on. The door opens. Golden light floods in. The person steps outside onto a sidewalk. They put in one earbud. The camera follows from behind as they start walking — slowly at first, then with purpose. The city unfolds around them.
>
> **SCENE 4 (15–22s):** Walking through a neighborhood (Capitol Hill, Seattle vibes — murals, coffee shops, tree-lined streets). The person looks up at buildings, notices things. They smile for the first time. A subtle warm glow appears around them as they walk — like the city is waking up to them. Quick cuts: a park, a fountain, a street they've never been on.
>
> **SCENE 5 (22–27s):** Two or three friends walking together through the same neighborhood, phones out, pointing at things, laughing. One of them looks at their phone screen — a glowing amber map with tiles lit up. They're exploring together. The warmth spreads.
>
> **SCENE 6 (27–30s):** Back to the original person, now home. Night. They open their phone one last time. A small glowing line on a dark map — their walk. They smile. Close the phone. Fade to black with a subtle amber glow remaining.

### 30-Second Version

> "73% of adults feel regularly lonely. 1 in 5 Americans move cities every 5 years and take 4 months to form a social circle. There are zero apps that put a friend in your ear on a walk AND a group on your map.
>
> Roam fixes both. For introverts like Jordan — stuck at a desk, flat, hasn't been outside today — Echo is the warm voice that says 'hey, let's go.' For extroverts like Kobe — just moved to Seattle, wants to explore — the group heat map turns walks into social discovery.

> We have a working demo, a deployed landing page, and we've talked to [N] people who all said the same thing: 'I would go outside more if I had a reason.' Roam is that reason."

## 4. What Problem Are You Solving? (Crystal Clear Version)

### The problem (one sentence):
> Young professionals — whether stuck indoors from screen fatigue or new to a city with no social circle — have no reason to go outside that doesn't feel like a chore.

### Why existing solutions fail (one sentence):
> Fitness apps track steps but don't create motivation; social apps connect you online but don't get you out the door.

### What Roam offers (one sentence):
> A voice companion (Echo) that makes solo walks feel like you're with a friend, and a shared heat map that makes group walks feel like exploration.

### Why now (one sentence):
> Voice AI just got good enough to feel like a real conversation, loneliness is at epidemic levels, and nobody has built a social walking app.
